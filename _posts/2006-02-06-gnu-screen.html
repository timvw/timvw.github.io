Another tool that belongs to my favorites is GNU Screen</a>. A couple of years ago i had a bad internet connection and when i was working on a remote shell i was always logged out and had to start over. I really hated that ;) Today i don't have this problem anymore, but i like to keep Irssi</a> (an IRC</a> user-agent) running, even when i'm not logged in. TIP Using Screen</a> is an article that gives a couple of other reasons to use it and explains how it works. The default settings suck if you also use GNU Emacs</a>. So i had to figure out a couple of keypresses. With "cat -v" i could easily see what those keypresses were. Here is my .screenrc file:</p>

##############################################

# Author: Tim Van Wassenhove

# Update: 2006-02-06 03:02

##############################################

startup_message off

escape ^Oo


bind i screen -t 'irssi'        3 irssi

bind v screen -t 'vim'          4 vim

bind m screen -t 'mutt'         5 mutt

bind b screen -t 'slrn belnet'  6 slrn -h news.belnet.be

bind r screen -t 'slrn php'     7 slrn -h news.php.net

bind e screen -t 'elinks'       8 elinks http://www.google.com
