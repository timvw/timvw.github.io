These days i use iTunes as media player. Unlike winamp this program does not seem to support system-wide hotkeys to control playback. This is the place where a productivity tool like AutoHotkey</a> comes into the rescue ;).</p>

I have configured the following keys:</p>

  • Move to next song: [Ctrl] + [Alt] + [Right]</li>
  • Move to previous song: [Ctrl] + [Alt] + [Left]</li>
  • Toggle play/pause: [Ctrl] + [Alt] + p</li>
  • Turn volume up: [Ctrl] + [Alt] + [Up]</li>
  • Turn volume down: [Ctrl] + [Alt] + [Down]</li>
    </ul>

    [code lang="plain"]
    ^!right::
    DetectHiddenWindows , On
    ControlSend , ahk_parent, ^{right}, iTunes ahk_class iTunes
    DetectHiddenWindows , Off
    return

    ^!left::
    DetectHiddenWindows , On
    ControlSend , ahk_parent, ^{left}, iTunes ahk_class iTunes
    DetectHiddenWindows , Off
    return

    ^!p::
    DetectHiddenWindows , On
    ControlSend , ahk_parent, {space}, iTunes ahk_class iTunes
    DetectHiddenWindows , Off
    return

    ^!up::
    DetectHiddenWindows , On
    ControlSend, ahk_parent, ^{UP}, iTunes ahk_class iTunes
    DetectHiddenWindows , Off
    return

    ^!down::
    DetectHiddenWindows , On
    ControlSend, ahk_parent, ^{DOWN}, iTunes ahk_class iTunes
    DetectHiddenWindows , Off
    return
    [/code]
