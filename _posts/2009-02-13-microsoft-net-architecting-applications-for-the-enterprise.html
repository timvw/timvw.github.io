Earlier this month i have read Microsoft .NET: Architecting Applications for the Enterprise</a>. This time i was pretty pleased to notice that the author, Dino Esposito</a>, did not fill the book with references to other books written by him. All in all the book painted a pretty accurate picture of enterprise application development that is obviously inspired by masterworks like: Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software</a>, Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture</a> and Domain-Driven Design: Tackling Complexity in the Heart of Software</a>.</p>

One remark though: I was a bit disappointed to see that the examples of unit tests used MSTEST. Why? Because it is such a broken product i find it hard to believe that for serious enterprise application development this library is actually usable.</p>
